Findings

Written analysis — the charts above are the evidence
  • Volume up, revenue down. In 2025 production kept climbing (a fourth FPSO, ONE GUYANA, started up in August and output hit 900,000 bpd by November) — yet oil revenue slipped, because Brent's decline outweighed the extra barrels. The result: 2026 is the first year Guyana's approved NRF withdrawal fell versus the prior year (US$2.37B vs US$2.46B). Price risk, not production risk, is now the fund's dominant variable.
  • From zero to a top-25 producer in six years. Annual average production went from 74,000 bpd (2020) to 616,000 bpd (2024), with capacity above 900,000 bpd by late 2025 — among the fastest offshore ramp-ups ever recorded.
  • The NRF is a flow-through, not yet a nest egg. Of roughly US$9.3B deposited since 2020, over US$6B has already been transferred to the national budget. The balance (~US$3.96B, May 2026) grows far slower than headline oil revenue suggests — a deliberate policy choice worth watching as production doubles.
